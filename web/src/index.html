About me

I'm a full professor (in computer science). My Ph.D. is from UNSW, Australia, 1995.

I teach graduate SE (spring) + automated SE (fall) Also, I am the curator of the PROMISE repository of SE data and the director of the RAISE lab (real world AI for SE).

In a former life I was the SE research chair at NASA, 2002-2003.

My Research

Q: What's the strangest, most inexplicable feature of the world?
A: That we can understand any of it, ever, at all.

To put that another way, how is it that apes as dumb as me have travelled to the moon, learned how to perform heart surgery, and built so many machines that work?

My only answer is that exists certain commonly occuring constraints that simplifies exploration of the world. And we can exploit those contrainst so simplify complex tasks like (1) building better privacy algorithms; (2) optimizing complex models, faster; (3) learn better models from data; (4) explain seemingly complex effects, very succinctly.

While exploring these ideas, I have won $7 million in competitive grants from:

  • The National Science Foundation;
  • NASA;
  • the US Defence Deparrment;
  • the US National Archives.
  • and other sources

Some of that funding has been in the form of "gifts" (i.e. undirected reserch); or research contracts (i.e. with fixed deliverables) or STTRs (Small Business Technology Transfer). For a full list, see here.

From that work, I've written over 230 refeered articles and, at the time of this writing, am one of the top 100 most-cited authors in software engineering (out of 80,000 researchers, in the last decade).
